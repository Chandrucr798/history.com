1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1631 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died on 17 June that year while giving birth to their 14th child, Gauhara Begum. Construction started in 1632, and the mausoleum was completed in 1648.

2. Petra

The Iron Age lasted between 1200 and 600 BC; during that time, the Petra area was occupied by the Edomites. The mountains of Petra provided a natural reservoir for water, making it a key trading hub for merchants dealing in wines, olive oil, and wood.
